Crappy people development is costing your company over €450k a year

(image inspo: 100soft)


I work with start ups on all things people leadership and development. Although the benefits of good people development benefits are wide reaching, there is one financial cost that is useful to evaluate. Both since it's so eye opening, and also because sometimes (sadly) it takes real money numbers to convince the people around us that it's worth investing in.

There's one thing to take away: a lack of effective people development is freakin' expensive.

Let's run through an example:
You're a 50 person start up.
With an average salary of €75k.
The average yearly turnover rate is 13-25%.
63% of those leave due to a lack of development opportunities.
And the cost of turnover is 150-200% of a persons salary.

Even on the conservative side, that means:
6.5 people will leave you this year.
4 will be from a lack of development opportunities.

...which will cost you €450,000.

How would you react to any other problem costing you €450k a year?

Whether you're in a position to do something about this problem yourself, or you're an advocate for good people development, use an example like this to justify a change that needs to happen today.
