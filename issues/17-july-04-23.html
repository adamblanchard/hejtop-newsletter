People don't have to grow.

Let's ditch the toxic idea that people have to grow.

It's a big assumption in the world of people management - and it is rarely challenged. Yes, we need to have loads of support and structures in place to help people do well, grow, learn new things. We also need space for everyone's individual needs to be heard. These two things are not mutually exclusiive.

I talk a lot about people, career and learning development, but I am not hyper-obsessed with performance, efficiency or pushing people as hard as possible. And I don't believe we should be.

A good people culture is a commitment to catering to individual needs, understanding that those can be very different and change a lot over time.

A useful way to reflect on why this is necessary is to take take a look at your own life & career:

There'll have been times you've been super stretching yourself, working mega hard, taking on loads of new stuff to learn, going outside your comfort zone - and asking for it! And that's great!

There'll also have been times where your "career" is not your top priority. You have relationship and family things to focus on. New kids. Breakups. Sickness. You might find a job role you're comfortable in, and want to chill in that space for a while to re-energise and work out what's next.

This is a human experience.

Careers are in flux, and that's ok. Our job is to appreciate the ups and the downs.

People do NOT need:

  • Constant pushing.
  • Constant stretch goals.
  • Their company to decide what's best for them.
What they DO need:
  • A genuinely people-first culture where their needs are unpacked and respected.
  • A multidimensional framework of opportunities, to explore in all sorts of directions and velocities.
  • A deep understanding that needs change and evolve, and a workplace that supports that.
If we approach our team genuinely as individuals, we can truly start to see what development they need, how they need it, and (importantly) when they need it.
