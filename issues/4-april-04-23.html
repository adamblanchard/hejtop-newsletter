Being a multiplier is hard

It's challenging to balance getting stuck in to help your team without inadvertantly taking away growth opportunities.

Here's some tips to multiply yourself to the max:

1. Consider this question deeply, “Would the team be able to manage x if I was on vacation for a day/week/month/year?”

2. Don’t be too quick to remove every blocker from the team. Too much squishes agency.

3. Adopt an automation mindset and consider how every impact you have as a manager could repeat itself without you.

4. Build feedback loops to help the team recognise and act on issues (both personal and processes).

5. Enable delegation by teaching frameworks for problem solving & decision making that don't involve you.

There’s two pieces to tackle here:
1. Expectation alignment
2. Priority boundaries

Chat with your team and manager openly, and share your ideas of how you will spend your time and why it's important now.

Since this changes over time, check in again with everyone on a frequent (e.g. monthly) basis.

Two options for setting boundaries to prioritise your time:
1. Time-based boundaries
2. Outcome-based boundaries

Time boundaries look like blocked time in your calendar. An hour each morning, Friday afternoons, or a time percentage to fill up each week. It provides you a focussed time to deprioritise everything else.

With outcome boundaries, you say e.g. “For this project, I’ll implement part x and be finished by date y”. This forces you to make time to complete this committed to outcome.

Keep sharing your evolving expectations and learnings with your team/manager while you find something that works.

Unhappy pay conversations are common. 64% of people paid at market rate believe they are underpaid.

Start with unpacking their unhappiness with a curious mindset and open questions.

Avoid questions that:
- Prove your own point.
- Make them feel wrong.
- Imply your opinions.

Learn about motivations and check out the BICEPS framework for human core needs (links below).

Steps to follow:
1. Unpack their needs & motivations.
2. Share more context for this specific payrise.
2. Brainstorm options together to tackle any gaps.
3. Choose an option and set goals to try out.

Note: If they want more money than your company can ever offer and they decide to leave, support them as best you can in finding a new job.
