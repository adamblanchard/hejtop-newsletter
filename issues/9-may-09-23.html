Good leadership starts with self-awareness

Start with reflecting on these points:

  1. Know what you're good at
  2. Know what you're bad at
  3. Know what you want to improve
  4. Know what to delegate/get help on
Take a piece of paper and write four big (or small üòÅ) lists covering each of the above.

Your next step is to talk about them with the people around you. That means your manager, your peers and your team.

They can help you evaluate them, share feedback, highlight any blind spots, give you ideas on improving them, and most importantly... build a shared understanding of YOU.

It's important to do this exericse and take these conversations with a mindset of transparency, vulnerability and honesty.

Understand yourself deeply and set accurate expecations for the role you take in your team.

From code to people

Your "thing" shifts from code to people as an engineering manager.

There's new hats you need to wear every day, and you'll need to know when to use each.

Mentoring:
This is where you'll be hands on helping, advising, and sharing your experience to teach and guide.
Wear this hat when people are looking for advice, or very stuck. Don't jump to this hat too quickly, otherwise you risk taking over and reducing people's space to grow themselves.

Coaching:
This is where you'll be asking questions and encouraging reflection to help people reach their goals.
Wear this hat by default. It allows you to help people unpack their challenges and find ways to move forward without making decisions for them.

Sponsorship:
Understanding and connecting people to opportunities for growth. You need to be their biggest advocate!
Wear this hat often to lift others up, especially to push people outside their comfort zone.

Be mindful of which hat is required for each situation you're in. It's even helpful to be transparent and say e.g. "I'm taking a coaching approach now. If you feel you need more hands on advice, let me know."

And keep practicing!
