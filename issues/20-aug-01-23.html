The management promotion problem

Management is not a promotion.
It's a lateral move onto a parallel track.

Your previous experience definitely helps you understand the team's context deeply, and loads of lessons you learned as an individual contributor (IC) will carry through and make you a great leader too.

However, framing this move as a promotion causes major problems.

Why?

Being a "contributor" is a totally different set of practical skills compared to being a manager.

That means you go from being a great IC to a mediocre manager.

Without recognising, accepting and working through these skill gaps, you'll remain a good IC and a bad manager. And hurt yourself, your career, and most problematic of all - other people.

How do we solve it? It's all about developing the right opportunities before promotions happen, and offering the right support afterwards:

1. Clear and interesting IC opportunities
First up, let's avoid the bored/unsure people trying out management just because they're not sure how else to grow.

Examples:

  • Clear IC growth track in your career framework
  • Equal pay opportunities across IC and manager levels
  • Enable easy parallel role shifts (e.g. between frontend, backend, infra teams)

2. Opportunities to explore management "pre-promotion"
Experiment with high learning, low risk stuff.

Examples:
  • Officially mentor multiple people
  • Leading project communication
  • Leading an existing hiring process

3. Incredibly solid, continuous support
One off training by an external provider is not enough!

Examples:
  • Internal leadership mentor to spar on approaches weekly
  • External coach to unpack challenges and grow
  • Continuous training program (e.g. monthly themes, external resources, discussion groups, actual time to practice)

With a dedicated and intentional approach to the process around promoting managers, we help people get clarity on whether it's what they want, if they are the right people, and make sure they are set up to be the best managers they can.
