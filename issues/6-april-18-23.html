Software development is a human system

Software is the output, but humans are the drivers. We are the ones that build, decide, get frustrated, have biases and preferences that shape it.

Emotions,
Relationships,
Communication,
Pyschological safety,
Leadership approach,
Organisational structure,
Environmental pressures,

...are the biggest deciding factors on the quality of software.

Unpack and intentionally invest in improving these human factors, for better humans and better software.

You are not your teams friend

As a manager, you should be kind, constructive, supportive, and be there for your team in every way you can.

Unfortunately, as long as you have the single power to control their salary and employment - don't assume their friendship.

Accepting this power dynamic is key to supporting your team and creating safety.

You might think you're making a small suggestion...
❌ NOPE, your words have huge weight!

You might think people can easily come to you with a problem...
❌ NOPE, they're scared how it will make them look!

You might think they'd tell you they were unhappy before finding another job...
❌ NOPE, they have themselves to protect!

You might not be friends, but it doesn't mean you can't be friendly. Just be mindful and transparent about this power dynamic, and you'll build stronger relationships with your team.

Saying thanks

When was the last time you got a "thank you" that you really felt?
When's the last time you said a meaningful thank you to someone on your team?

It sounds small, and it's easy to dismiss, but it can make a huge difference to how recognised and appreciated people feel.

It's a recurring theme in people leadership: simple and consistent actions make a huge impact. It's not only big iniatives or changes that make a difference.

Try it out today:
Think of someone who's made a difference to you recently, big or small, and go and share a meaningful thanks.
