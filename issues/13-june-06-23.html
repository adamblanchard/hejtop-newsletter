Feeling-driven goals

Everyone talks about goals being super objective, business driven and quite honestly... boring. Personal goals can be measured by FEELINGS. Which are way more motivating.

Having clear outcomes is important. The problem is, it's hard to start from a place of objectivity. So start with how someone feels!

Here's an example:

"I'm not a good enough engineer because I don't understand anything the team is talking about in our planning meetings."

Ask them what it would look like if they overcame it...

"I'd feel confident to ask relevant questions and offer suggestions".

Now the feeling is understood, work back to find some measurable outcomes...

"Be able to ask 2 relevant questions every planning meeting".
"Be able to offer 1 suggestion every planning meeting".

The importance is not in these specific outcomes themselves, but the feedback loop it provides people to understand their progress. You can use this insight to help reflect more tangibly on how they feel towards the end of the goal.

Unpacking motivations and ambitions

Want to help your team members grow? Put the online courses and review processes down (for now).

Start by understanding these three things:

1. Motivators
Don't just ask them. Talk about their life and career so far. Unpack key decisions and understand why.

2. Career pinnacles
Not just at your company. Help people step outside the box and learn about their long term, deeper desires for their career.

2. Skills
Evaluate their current strengths, weaknesses and gaps. Start with self evaluation, add your own input on top. Bonus: Get 360 feedback.

Now you have the foundation for building a solid growth plan together, with motivating goals that get them closer to their long term ambitions while filling any tangible skill gaps along the way.
