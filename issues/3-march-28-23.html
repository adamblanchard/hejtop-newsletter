Do you know how you learn most effectively?

I mean personally. If you need to learn a new skill or absorb a whole bunch of new knowledge, do you have a good grasp of your own learning strategies?

Unfortunately, this curiosity is taught out of many of us through school. Imagine you aren't much of an auditory learner, but you're forced to sit through 6 hours of back to back lectures a day. Not the best way to inspire people to own their own development!

There's a bunch of learning style theories, the simplest and most common being the VARK theory. It categorises learners as visual, auditory, reading/writing or kinesthetic.

I'm definitely a reading/writing and visual learner. Getting stuff down on paper helps me shape my own thoughts - especially with drawings!

I hand draw all the illustrations in the hejtop newsletter (ipad + apple pencil + procreate are a magical combo!). If you're a visual learner like me, I hope they help solidify the ideas I share in here as much as they help me.

Here's an underappreciated way to build trust with your team

Your reactions & decisions should not be based on whether you’re in a good mood or not.

They should be consitent, predictable and agnostic to your current state & context.

Multiply your management impact and let your team shine without you

(Thanks to Ignatius for the diagram suggestion!)

”Management by multiplying” means bringing the best out in the people around you to achieve more.

"Management by addition”, on the other hand, means adding more time/effort/people to achieve more.

Management by multiplying gives your team the space grow whilst getting yourself out of the way.

Developers’ growth takes a big knockback when their manager changes

It will always happen at some point. Managers quit, get new jobs, change teams, get laid off.

Devs have to start from zero with their new manager. Building trust, sharing motivations, goals, ideals, long term ambitions, personal challenges.

Any existing plans and expectations are lost. Whether that's working towards a promotion, promise to work on an upcoming project or actioning some performance feedback.

Help minimise these impacts by maintaining a “Personal Summary” page together with everyone on your team. It should summarise their current motivations, level, career ambitions and any existing action plans.

Besides it being useful to keep an ongoing summary of the above, it becomes super invaluable in any handover situation.
