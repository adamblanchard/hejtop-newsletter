There's always a reason to lead from a position of fear

Maybe you have a new role. A bad manager. A new risky project. A scary client. Super ambitious goals. Non-negotiable financial targets. Uncertain economy. Losing your job.

It's up to you when you let fear drive your decision making. It's hard, but it is a choice.

Not sure if you are falling into this trap? Evaluate a few of your last big decisions by repeatadly asking yourself why, and see if the deeper reasons sound like insecurity, anxiety and fear.

A paper diving into how we respond to fear. Spoiler: When we are full of fear, we are always unlikely to react in a measured way.

Lacking confidence when making decisions?

Decisions are either reversible or irreversible. Or somewhere on that scale.

Reversible decisions are two way doors.
e.g. "Let's make a prototype, and if it doesn't work we can try the next idea".
Prioritise these decisions and make them quickly.

Irreversible decisions are one way doors.
e.g. "Let's invest all our money in this new strategy".
Choose this less often and take more care.

Consider which side of the scale you're sitting on, and if you're ever feeling stuck, find a reversible way forward. And repeat.

Ever lose track of discussions and decisions that were made?

It's hard to be note taker in meetings and conversations, especially when you're trying to actively listen and also take part as well.

It's a skill worth practicing. Capturing key points, decisions and actions will not only help you remember, but help bring clarity to everyone else too.

I keep them all on one page in Notion, ready to quickly add new notes in a structured way with a click of a button. It's ordered by time, and super easy to search back.
